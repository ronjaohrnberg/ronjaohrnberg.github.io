The Initial Command (1987)

The album starts strong with The State, which is not the most rad FLA song there is, not even close, but it is energetic and enjoyable nontheless with very nice sounds. Insanity Lurks Nearby continues smoothly with the same vibe and atmosphere, even slightly easing and relaxing from the opening song. Intelligence Dream is easy on the ears, very catchy, but it does lack some impact that the more striking songs have. Casualties stays mainly on the same lane, some sounds and the more melodic parts break the trend, which is welcome at this point. No big surprises though. Aussgang Zum Himmel goes for a different vibe, and these vibes and sounds are strong. Definitely a gem with good timing, since this is not a melodic song, so after the midway point is a great spot for this one. Nine Times is quite different from its precedents, not necessarily in a good way, as the previous song would have allowed a transition to a more insane direction, but it is definitely a FLA song, and not a bad one either. The song would possibly work rather well on a dancefloor, as sounds and vocals work excellently with the groovy rhythm. Black March doesn't leave a strong impression, in good or bad. It continues smooth after the previous one, but never rises up to excellence. No Control is a great finishing track, the atmosphere and the sounds are lovely. It is similar to the other songs, but with more energy.

The album leans to the more relaxed side of FLA productions. The songs work well together and the album stays coherent with some songs mixing things up a bit. No Control, Aussgang Zum Himmel, and Casualties are banging.

Gashed Senses & Crossfire (1989)

Starting with No Limit, the albums kicks on with tight energy and solid rhythm. The song is very even with good energy, not a true banger, but never boring. Antisocial has a great atmosphere and energy, vocals are interesting and sounds - especially the instrumentals in the background - as well. Gives me the chills. Hypocrisy has nice instrumentals as well, sounds slightly like a soundtrack during a scary scene, but the song does not rise to greatness. The strings and vocals work together well, however. Shutdown has great vocals and the overall vibe is nice, very melodic, but it does not leave a strong impression in the end. Prayer is a tight song, the sounds are excellent and the atmosphere is strong. Digital Tension Dementia moves with energy. The rhythm, the vocals, and the sounds all scream FLA, not from the most banging end, but not from the worst either. Big Money continues similarly, but with better rhythm and vocals. Bloodsport has excellent sounds and energy, quite banging indeed. Restless and lovely song, this too is emblematically FLA. Foolsgame continues the trend, but with more melody and less impact. Sedation ends the album with great vocals and atmosphere.

The album is quite coherent, with mostly good tracks and some rather unnoticeable ones here and there. I'd highlight Antisocial, Prayer, Big Money, and Bloodsport as great tracks from this one.
